3. RESEARCH PURPOSE AND OBJECTIVES

The data will be used exclusively for the project: "Ancestry-Stratified Genomic Risk Factors for Atrial Fibrillation" (Ethics Approval: HRI-IRB-2026-0088). The approved purpose is:

a) To identify ancestry-specific genetic variants associated with atrial fibrillation risk across four ancestral groups.
b) To develop and validate polygenic risk scores for atrial fibrillation that perform equitably across diverse populations.
c) To assess the interaction between genetic risk factors and clinical risk factors (age, hypertension, BMI) in diverse populations.

Data use is strictly limited to these objectives. No secondary uses, re-analysis for other conditions, or linkage to external datasets are planned without separate ethics approval and DAC authorization.

4. ETHICS OVERSIGHT AND CONSENT COMPATIBILITY

This project has been reviewed and approved by the Helix Research Institute Institutional Review Board (Ethics Protocol: HRI-IRB-2026-0088, approved February 5, 2026). A copy of the full IRB approval letter is enclosed.

We have confirmed with the IGDA data custodians that the IGDA-CARD-2023-0412 dataset was collected under broad research consent that explicitly permits secondary use for cardiovascular genomics research. Our proposed use falls squarely within the scope of the original consent. We have reviewed the dataset's consent documentation and confirmed compatibility with our research objectives. No re-consent is required.

5. DATA HANDLING AND SECURITY

All data will be stored and processed exclusively on the Helix Research Institute's Secure Computing Environment (SCE), which meets NIST SP 800-53 High baseline security controls. Specific measures include:

Physical safeguards:
- Data center with biometric access control and 24/7 monitoring
- No removable media permitted
- Visitor log maintained

Organizational safeguards:
- Role-based access control (RBAC) — only listed team members have access
- Mandatory annual security training for all personnel
- Access logs reviewed monthly by the Information Security Officer

Technological safeguards:
- Encryption at rest: AES-256
- Encryption in transit: TLS 1.3
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) via institutional SSO
- Regular automated backups to encrypted off-site storage
- Intrusion detection system (IDS) with real-time alerting
- Annual third-party penetration testing
