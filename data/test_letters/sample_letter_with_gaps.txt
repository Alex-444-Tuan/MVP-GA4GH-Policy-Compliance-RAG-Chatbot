Dear Members of the Data Access Committee,

I am writing to formally request access to the Multi-Ancestry Whole Genome Sequencing Dataset (NGBC-WGS-2024-0847) held by the National Genomic Biobank Consortium. This letter outlines the purpose, scope, and conditions of the proposed data use.

1. PRINCIPAL INVESTIGATOR AND RESEARCH TEAM

I, Dr. Sarah Chen, serve as the Principal Investigator for this project. I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Computational Genomics at Brookfield University with 12 years of experience in population genetics and statistical genomics.

The research team includes:
- Dr. James Okafor, Postdoctoral Fellow (Computational Genomics)
- Ms. Priya Sharma, PhD Candidate (Biostatistics)
- Mr. David Liu, Research Assistant (Data Engineering)

2. RESEARCH PURPOSE AND OBJECTIVES

The requested data will be used for a research project titled "Polygenic Risk Score Transferability Across Ancestral Populations" (IRB Protocol #BU-2026-0312). The primary objectives are:

a) To evaluate the predictive accuracy of existing polygenic risk scores for Type 2 Diabetes across five ancestral populations represented in the dataset.

b) To develop novel cross-ancestry PRS methods that improve prediction equity for underrepresented populations.

c) To identify ancestry-specific genetic variants contributing to differential PRS performance.

This research aligns with the GA4GH principle of ensuring that the benefits of genomic science extend equitably to diverse populations.

3. DATA REQUESTED

We request access to the following components of NGBC-WGS-2024-0847:

- Whole genome sequencing data (VCF files) for all participants
- Associated phenotype data (Type 2 Diabetes status, BMI, age, sex, self-reported ancestry)
- Quality control metrics and sequencing metadata

We estimate requiring the data for a period of 36 months from the date of access approval.

4. DATA HANDLING AND SECURITY

All data will be stored on Brookfield University's High-Performance Computing Cluster (HPCC), which is located in a physically secure data center with restricted badge access. The computing environment uses encrypted storage and access is controlled via institutional Single Sign-On (SSO) with multi-factor authentication.

Only the named research team members listed above will have access to the individual-level data.

5. ANTICIPATED OUTCOMES AND DISSEMINATION

We expect the research to produce:
- 2-3 peer-reviewed publications in journals such as Nature Genetics or the American Journal of Human Genetics
- Open-source software tools for cross-ancestry PRS calculation
- Presentations at the American Society of Human Genetics (ASHG) annual meeting

All summary-level results and methods will be made publicly available. Individual-level data will not be published or shared.

6. INSTITUTIONAL APPROVAL

This project has been reviewed and approved by the Brookfield University Institutional Review Board (IRB Protocol #BU-2026-0312, approved January 10, 2026).

7. AGREEMENT TO TERMS

I agree to use the data solely for the purposes described above and to comply with all applicable regulations governing genomic data use.

I acknowledge that the data represents contributions from human participants and commit to treating it with the respect and care that this entails.
